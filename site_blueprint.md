# Site Blueprint

The homepage should function as a **self-contained academic calling card** rather than as a conventional portfolio. Its purpose is to state, with clarity and restraint, who David Goh is, what he is trying to do, and where a reader should go next. The governing tone is scholarly, purposeful, and text-led. The page should therefore present him as someone working toward shaping education through leadership, building, and writing, while making clear that his technical and mathematical work is in service of that larger vocation.

The visual approach should follow the **Scholarly Marginalia** direction already chosen in `ideas.md`. In practice, that means a paper-like light background, dark editorial typography, narrow rules, modest accents, and an asymmetric composition that reads more like a finely typeset statement than a product homepage. The page should feel minimal because it has been disciplined, not because it is unfinished.

| Section | Purpose | Content direction |
|---|---|---|
| Intro | Establish identity immediately | Name, current academic position, and a short opening statement about shaping education through leadership, building, and writing |
| Profile | Give the reader a compact intellectual and institutional picture | MScAC (Applied Mathematics) at the University of Toronto, Singapore Public Service Commission (Teaching Service) Scholar, mathematician and builder, future public-sector leadership path |
| Links | Provide clear exits without clutter | LinkedIn, GitHub, and optionally email later |
| Selected work | Show evidence of building and inquiry | Three visible GitHub projects for now, with space deliberately reserved for four recent projects or preprints to be added later |
| Closing note | End with direction rather than self-promotion | A brief sentence about mathematics, public purpose, and education |

The opening copy should be built around the user's own language. A working formulation is that David Goh wants to be a force that shapes education, and to that end he intends to lead, build, and write. That line should anchor the page, but the supporting text should temper ambition with seriousness by emphasizing principle, mathematical training, and public purpose.

The projects area should be intentionally split into two registers. The first register should present currently visible public work drawn from GitHub. The second register should present a reserved space for the four recent projects or preprints that are not yet on GitHub or LinkedIn. That reserved space should look deliberate and editorial, so that the site already feels complete even before those entries are filled in.

The result should be a one-page site that answers four questions quickly and well: who this person is, what he is trying to do, what institutions and commitments shape him, and where to go for evidence of his work.
